ðŸŽ¯ About Activation Functions

Activation functions are mathematical functions that determine the output of a neural network node. They introduce non-linearity into the network, allowing it to learn complex patterns and relationships in data.

Without activation functions, neural networks would simply be linear transformations, making them incapable of solving complex problems like image recognition or natural language processing.

Sigmoid

Smooth, S-shaped curve. Output range: (0, 1). Can suffer from vanishing gradients.

ReLU

Simple and effective. Output: max(0, x). Most popular for hidden layers.

Tanh

Similar to sigmoid but centered at zero. Output range: (-1, 1).

Leaky ReLU

Variation of ReLU that allows small negative values to prevent dead neurons.

Swish

Self-gated activation function. Often outperforms ReLU in deep networks.

GELU

Gaussian Error Linear Unit. Used in modern transformers like BERT and GPT.

ELU

Exponential Linear Unit. Smooth everywhere and can produce negative outputs.
